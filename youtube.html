Conclusion

The arrival of Christian missionaries left the Indgenous populations of Canada with lasting effects on their identity. The Haudenosaunee and Cree peoples were only two groups of the over 50 distinc nations whose spiritual traditions and values were disrupted due to missionary influence. Most groups adapted to Christianity in some way or form, be it a hybrid worldview with Christianity or preservation through adaptation, christian missionaries forced Indgenous groups to face cultural erasure, and disconnection from their traditional beliefs. The effects that missionaries had on Indgenous people throughout the world aren't just historical-they continue to shape how Indigenous communities are forced to navigate their identities and spiritual practices in Canada.
